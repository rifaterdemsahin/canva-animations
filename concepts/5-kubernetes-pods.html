Concept: Kubernetes Pods & Nodes

üß† The Concept

In Kubernetes, a `Node` is a worker machine (like a server). A `Pod` is the smallest deployable unit and runs on a Node. Kubernetes automatically schedules Pods to run on available Nodes in the cluster.

üìù Steps in Canva

  1. Nodes: Create large squares to represent Nodes.
  2. Pods: Create smaller circles to represent Pods.
  3. Scheduling: Animate the Pod circles floating down and landing inside the Node squares using the "Drift" animation.

üñ±Ô∏è Process in Detail

Follow these clicks to create the animation in Canva:

  • Step 1: Setup. Create a new "Video" design.
  • Step 2: Create Nodes. In "Elements", add two large square shapes at the bottom of the canvas. These are your Nodes.
  • Step 3: Create Pods. Add several smaller circle shapes at the top of the canvas. These are your Pods.
  • Step 4: Animate Scheduling.
    • Select one of the Pod circles.
    • Click "Animate" and choose the "Drift" animation.
    • In the animation settings, you can draw a path for the Pod to follow, guiding it into one of the Node squares.
    • Repeat this for all Pods, scheduling them into the available Nodes.
  • Step 5: Adjust Timing. Use the timeline to stagger the animations so the pods appear to be scheduled one after another.
